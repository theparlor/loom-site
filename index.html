Awareness & record

Every session orients instead of re-briefing.

Loom is the cross-session awareness substrate. Every agent, sidecar, and cron job reads and writes to a shared coherence surface — so the next session inherits what the last one knew, not a blank slate. SQLite-WAL backed. Seven verbs. No lock contention by construction.

The problem it solves

Every session runs blind to its siblings. That's the blindspot Loom closes.

A sidecar kicks off research another sidecar answered twenty minutes ago. A cron job writes a signal no running session will see for hours. A fresh Main has no way to ask "what are my siblings doing right now?" The coherence layer holds long-term artifact state, but had no primitive for live cross-session awareness — what's alive, what just sealed, what's parked waiting on an answer. Loom is that primitive. When it works, sidecars deduplicate silently. Deferred questions become a queue, not a halt. Sessions cite each other.

The dedup happened. The ledger proved it. A sidecar avoided re-running work another session had already written — not because it was told to, but because Loom gave it the priming to see.
How you use it

Seven verbs. One shared surface. Every session class welcome.

1

Register & orient

A session announces itself and receives a starter projection of live siblings — scored by engagement, product, and working-directory overlap. Arrive knowing what's already in motion.

2

Write & heartbeat

Append decisions, signals, questions, and results to your own thread. Mark yourself alive every 30 seconds. Park a blocking question and carry on — it surfaces in the next Main's priming context automatically.

3

Seal & hand off

When work is done, write a final selvedge record. Topography reads the last N selvedges at its COMPACT seam and produces one durable handoff. Live state transitions to compressed state without manual transfer.

What makes it different

The conservation law: threads are writer-scoped. Cross-thread writes are rejected.

The rule the whole product is built around

Lock contention disappears by construction.

Most shared-state systems fight over writes. Loom avoids the fight by design: every session writes only to its own thread. Cross-thread writes are rejected at the API surface — not by a runtime guard that could be bypassed, but by the port contract itself. The result is two reading modes that are never confused:

Live siblings

Currently-active sessions with thread heads — ranked, filtered, and ready for priming context at session start.

Sealed selvedges

Final records from sessions that just closed — the trailing warp that Topography compresses into durable handoffs.
